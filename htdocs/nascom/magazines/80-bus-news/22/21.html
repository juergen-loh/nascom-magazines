I would also like to correct the impression that a Biphase signal contains only frequencies between half the Baud rate and the Baud rate itself. By chance I had been experimenting with FFT’s (Fast Fourier Transforms) and had calculated spectra for NRI. Biphase and Biphase-M encoded random data streams shortly before the arrival of issue 3. These show that in fact the spectra of both Biphase signals reach a peak at the Baud rate, and have minima at 0 frequency (ie DC) and twice the Baud rate. NRZ on the other hand has a minimum at the Baud rate but is not DC free and so cannot be used for taping data. This is not to say however that Biphase data cannot be recovered after passing it through a bandpass filter such as a tape recorder. (My FFT program is written in Tape Pascal 4, and takes about 30 seconds to calculate a 512 point spectrum.)
